El plano de los dioses y semidioses

Los habitantes de Theros trabajan duro para ganarse el pan y el agua de cada día, pero nada de eso les impide rezar y hacer ofrendas a sus dioses patrones. Entidades divinas y etéreas, los dioses supervisan el funcionamiento de Theros y los elementos, cada uno movido por una ideología propia:

<

>

Heliod

Heliod is the god of the sun, embodying justice, pride, and the illuminating power of light. Revered as a symbol of law and righteousness, he is a steadfast protector of order but also harbors a blinding arrogance. Heliod's radiance shines over all, guiding his followers with unwavering belief in his divine authority.
However, his brilliance casts deep shadows, revealing his intolerance for dissent. Heliod's worshipers value discipline, honor, and devotion, striving to uphold his vision of a just world.

"The Champion reached for a stone, and Heliod placed the sun into her hand."

—The Theriad

Thassa

Thassa is the god of the sea and is also associated with aquatic creatures and the secrets of the briny deep. She is also the god of ancient knowledge, murmurs, gradual change, introspection, vast distances, long voyages, and far-ranging searches. Thassa can also be described as the god of patterns, such as tides, currents, ripples in water, and even the passage of time.

“Those who incur Thassa’s anger will be swallowed whole by her waves, or torn asunder by her bident.”

—Thrasios, triton hero

Erebos

Erebos rules over death and the Underworld. As he was vanished the precise moment he was born, he is also the god of misfortune, ill fate, begrudging acceptance, envy, and bitterness. Because the dead leave their earthly riches behind, Erebos has become associated with that wealth, as well as with the abundance of (to him, pretty but valueless) gold in his realm.

“No matter who their fickle hearts worship, all mortals belong to one god in the end.”

—Iadorna, death priest of Erebos

Purphoros

Purphoros is associated with the forge, the restless earth, and the fire. He rules the creative force of heat and energy in the souls of sentient beings: this energy is chaotic, something that needs to be harnessed and shaped by labor and passion. For this reason, Purphoros is also the god of artisans, obsession, and the cycle of creation and destruction. His forge burns in the heart of Mount Velus, attracting dragons with its blistering heat.

“Defeating the Meletians was not so difficult. I needed only to move a mountain.”

—Eocles, oracle of Purphoros

Nylea

Nylea is associated with the hunt, the seasons, and the forests. Her reign over the hunt extends to predation and hunger, while with her rule over the seasons, she is also a patron of metamorphosis and rebirth. At the center of her grove, there is a chrysalis, in which Nylea usually sleeps to receive strength and peace.

“It is not yours to hunt, mortal. Be content with its beauty alone.”

—Nylea, god of the hunt
