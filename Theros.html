Where Godhood and Mankind Intertwine

Theros is a mythological plane in which the influence of deities shapes the fate of its inhabitants. These gods are real and play a central role in its culture, each ruling over different aspects of the world, such as the sea, the sun, and war. They are not distant, abstract beings but active participants in the lives of their followers, often manifesting in physical forms and directly influencing events.

Theros is also home to mighty heroes who undertake epic quests, facing legendary creatures and overcoming daunting challenges in service to the gods. This plane is full of powerful enchantments, and its citizens rely heavily on divine favor to achieve greatness. The land itself is diverse, with sprawling cities, vast oceans, towering mountains, and dark forests filled with ancient secrets.

The plane’s aesthetic and lore is rich in classical architecture, heroic tales of heroes, and divine struggles. Magic on Theros is deeply intertwined with the mythological themes of fate and the defiance of it, earning glory, and the eternal conflict between good and evil. It’s a world where gods weave the fabric of destiny, and mortals can only aspire to be part of their grand stories.

Heliod

Heliod is the god of the sun, embodying justice, pride, and the illuminating power of light. Revered as a symbol of law and righteousness, he is a steadfast protector of order but also harbors a blinding arrogance. Heliod's radiance shines over all, guiding his followers with unwavering belief in his divine authority.
However, his brilliance casts deep shadows, revealing his intolerance for dissent. Heliod's worshipers value discipline, honor, and devotion, striving to uphold his vision of a just world.

"The Champion reached for a stone, and Heliod placed the sun into her hand."

—The Theriad

Thassa

Thassa is the god of the sea, and she embodies change, mystery, and the inexorable power of water. Serene yet unpredictable, she governs tides, currents, and the secrets hidden beneath the waves.
She values patience and adaptability, reflecting the ever-shifting nature of the ocean. Though calm on the surface, her wrath is as devastating as a storm.
Her followers revere her as a protector of sailors and a guide to hidden truths, striving to embrace her fluid, enigmatic wisdom.

“Those who incur Thassa’s anger will be swallowed whole by her waves, or torn asunder by her bident.”

—Thrasios, triton hero

Erebos

Erebos, the god of the dead, presides over the underworld and embodies inevitability, sorrow, and acceptance of mortality. Shrouded in shadow, he governs the cycle of life and death, ensuring that no soul escapes its destined end.
He is a figure of grim mercy and quiet authority, offering solace to those who accept their fate and punishment to those who defy it. His followers embrace the inevitability of death, finding strength in life’s fleeting nature and the unyielding balance he represents.

“No matter who their fickle hearts worship, all mortals belong to one god in the end.”

—Iadorna, death priest of Erebos

Purphoros

Purphoros, the god of the forge, embodies creation, passion, and the raw power of fire. A master craftsman, he shapes the world through molten metal and unyielding flame, representing both destructive chaos and inspired artistry.
He values creativity and individuality, encouraging his followers to forge their own paths and leave lasting marks on the world. His temper is as fiery as his domain, but his creations are unmatched in beauty and power, reflecting his divine brilliance and restless spirit.

“Defeating the Meletians was not so difficult. I needed only to move a mountain.”

—Eocles, oracle of Purphoros

Nylea

Nylea, the god of the hunt, embodies nature, wildlife, and the untamed beauty of the natural world. She is the protector of forests and beasts, revered as a symbol of balance and the cycle of life.
She values independence, harmony, and respect for nature’s laws, often clashing with those who exploit or harm the wilds. Her followers, hunters and druids alike, strive to live in sync with the land, drawing strength and wisdom from her tranquil yet fierce presence.

“It is not yours to hunt, mortal. Be content with its beauty alone.”

—Nylea, god of the hunt
