The Planes of Magic: The Gathering

Planes are the distinct worlds that make up the multiverse in Magic: The Gathering. Each plane is a self-contained universe with its own unique geography, inhabitants, history, cultures, and magical rules. They are diverse in every sense—ranging from the eldritch horror of New Phyrexia to the celestial beauty of Theros or the untamed wilderness of Ikoria.

Planes are separated by the Blind Eternities, a chaotic void of raw energy that only planeswalkers—individuals with a rare Spark—can traverse. Planeswalkers can travel between these worlds, exploring their wonders and becoming embroiled in their conflicts. Most inhabitants of a plane, however, are unaware of the multiverse and live their lives confined to their own world.

Magic is integral to the identity of each plane, almost defining it all on its own. For instance, Capenna is a land which runs on a substance called 'Halo', while Ikoria's wild, raw aether triggers endless mutations on its wildlife. Planes are often shaped by their histories, gods, or key figures, and they serve as the settings for many of the game’s stories and mechanics.
