A Gilded Plane of Crime and Glamour

New Capenna is a sprawling plane defined by its shimmering decadence and gritty underworld. Built atop the ruins of an ancient civilization, the city is a marvel of Art Deco-inspired architecture, blending luxury with shadows of its mysterious past.
The key to its power lies in Halo, a magical substance that fuels both the opulence of the elite and the ambitions of the desperate, providing inhumane strength, supernatural healing and enhanced senses.

The plane is ruled by five rival crime families: the conniving Obscura, the zealous Brokers, the ambitious Maestros, the relentless Riveteers, and the flamboyant Cabaretti. Each family wields distinct magic and influence, vying for control over New Capenna’s streets and secrets. Beneath the glamour of their speakeasies, skyscrapers, and lavish galas, lies a brutal struggle for dominance, where alliances are fragile, and betrayal is commonplace.

Inhabitants of New Capenna walk a fine line between survival and indulgence, their lives shaped by the city’s widespread corruption and inevitable allure.
For visitors, it is a world of intrigue, a labyrinth of power plays and moral dilemmas where everyone puts their own interests first. Whether drawn by its beauty or its danger, they must find a way to navigate a web of deception where ambition rules, and nothing is ever as it seems.

The Obscura

Led by the sphinx-demon Raphine, the Obscura are talented wizards and mystics who use their power to deceive and blackmail, using distractions, illusions, and hidden mechanisms. They orchestrate scenarios and manipulate outcomes to their benefit. They deal in prophecy, fortune telling, and esoteric communion with dreams and the realm of the dead, all the while endeavoring to maintain a facade of normalcy in their everyday lives which allows them to run their schemes without disruption.

“Feed the rumor mills with delicious treachery. Bad blood between those two is a good deal for us.”

—Kamiz, Obscura Oculus

The Brokers

Led by the demon-aven Falco Spara, the originator of the Brokers's contract-weaving abilities, the Brokers are a demonic law firm that secretly believes a doomsday prophecy that when the halo dries up, New Capenna will fail. They maintain public law offices where they handle mundane matters like property disputes and physical grievances. But their aggressive solicitors have a dubious reputation for being everywhere and showing up at every crisis in the city.

"Brokers agents defend their charges by all means available, from intricate logic to blunt force trauma."

The Cabaretti

Managed by the demon-cat Jetmir and his adopted daughter Jinnie Fay, and raised from the druids and bards of Old Capenna, the Cabaretti are the engine that fuels New Capenna's celebrations, festivals, and parties, dictating both pop culture and the trends that move the city's elite. Opulent revelers, the Cabaretti run dance halls, concert venues, and the finest restaurants up and down the city's boroughs. They are the glitterati of the city and everyone wants to be invited to their feasts and dance halls. They use ancient magic to sway the opinion of the masses and keep the halo flowing.

“Control the Halo and you control the whole city.”

—Jetmir

The Maestros

Led by the vampire Anhelo, the Maestros are New Capenna's premier assassins, vampiric professional killers who see a contract not just as business but as a chance to perform. They maintain a warped and self-serving view of their work: they are saviors of the city, philanthropists beyond compare, and are ruthless in their pursuit of the fame and cultural cache that they believe they deserve. They will kill without a thought or moment of regret if it means achieving their goals.

“In the assassination trade, no one lives to make more than one mistake.”

—Anhelo, the Painter

The Riveteers

Managed by the steady claw of the demon-dragon Ziatora, and hailing from the artisans that built the kingdoms of Old Capenna, the Riveteers are the major power of the Caldaia, the mortal builders of New Capenna, and the keepers of the largest stockpile of Halo in the city. They are a rowdy, action-first, move-fast-and-break-things crime family whose members are not afraid of fighting hammer and nail to accomplish their goals. Once a union of workers, the Riveteers still hold to many of the values that once organized them: loyalty, strength in numbers, and mutual support.

“It’s too quiet tonight. Go make trouble.”

—Ziatora
