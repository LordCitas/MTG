The Land of The Kami

Kamigawa is a plane where the physical world and the spirit realm are intricately intertwined, with the influence of kami, powerful spirits, shaping the lives of its inhabitants. The people of Kamigawa coexist with these spirits, worshipping them in hopes of gaining their blessings, protection, and guidance. The plane is divided into multiple regions, ranging from lush forests to bustling cities.

In Kamigawa, technology and magic coexist in a delicate balance. While the ancient traditions of the samurai and the mystical power of the kami dominate much of the landscape, modern innovations, such as advanced technology and cybernetic enhancements, are beginning to emerge. This fusion of the spiritual and the technological is a defining feature of Kamigawa, especially in its more urbanized areas.

The struggle between the mortal world and the kami is a central theme in Kamigawa's story. Conflict arises as the mortal realm and the spirit realm clash, often leading to dramatic events and shifts in the balance of power. Kamigawa is a rich, vibrant world where ancient traditions meet futuristic innovation, creating a unique and ever-evolving landscape of magic, technology, and spiritual conflict.

Ao

Ao is the reincarnation of Yosei, the divine dragon that was guardian of Kamigawa's plains and the city of Eiganjo. Haunted by the memories of his violent death when he was torn in half in a battle against O-Kagachi, Ao refused to assume the role of Eiganjo's protector again after the city was rebuilt and tries to distance himself from mortal affairs as much as possible.
On occasions, though, he will break his own vows, unable to stand by and do nothing when an extreme injustice is being committed.

“A visionary looks at rubble and sees not only what was, but also what could be.”

—Onyx-Eye, architect of Eiganjo

Kairi

The reincarnation of Keiga, the divine dragon of islands, Kairi continued to protect Minamo until Minamo's decline late in the Shattered States Era.
When Otawara began to rise as the preeminent center of learning and innovation, they sought out Kairi for the dragon's protection, but Kairi was suspicious of the moonfolks' intentions and refused.
Nowadays he spends most of his time exploring the oceans and skies of Kamigawa, discovering mysteries that he keeps to himself. He is rarely seen outside of festivals or ritual events.

“Center of the Sea, Eye of the World, Shrine of Enlightenment. Seek it for answers. Seek it for healing. Seek it and return transformed.”

—Scroll fragment from the ruins of Minamo

Junji

Unlike many of the other divine dragons, Kokusho didn't die during the Kami War, but was killed decades afterwards guarding the denizens of Takenuma against an oni attack.
When he was reincarnated as Junji, he chose to distance himself completely from the swamp, abandoning Takenuma and leaving its inhabitants to fend for themselves. He took up residence in the canals snaking through the Towashi Undercity, his presence a constant, silent warning that keeps Reckoner gangs from letting their turf wars get too out of hand.

“We are recalling all forces from the Takenuma Swamp. We lose men daily to kami attacks, and it seems our defeats only encourage them further.”

—General Takeno, letter to Lord Konda

Atsushi

Atsushi is the reincarnation of Ryusei, the divine dragon of mountains. Out of all the divine dragons, Atsushi has most fully embraced her reincarnation.
Despite the fact that she never died, she chose to be reborn along with the other dragons and take on a new form. Most of the time, she's genial and happy-go-lucky, though she's been known to fly into a destructive rage whenever someone manages to trigger her flashfire temper.
She continues to act as guardian for the Sokenzan mountains and is a frequent visitor at Sokenzanshi, where her presence is always welcomed enthusiastically.

“Among the artisans of Sokenzan City, reusing every scrap of metal is a point of pride.”

Kura

Kura is the reincarnation of Jugan, former protector of the Jukai Forest. She was resurrected in her original form by the orochi but realized that with most of the other dragons dead, the balance of nature was in a precarious position.
She collected the essences of all the dragons and then placed them inside an egg, which eventually hatched the reincarnations of all five dragons.
As Kura, she continues to act as guardian of Jukai Forest, protecting it fiercely against incursions by all, especially the encroaching growth of Towashi, Kamigawa's booming metropolis.

“The soul of Jukai reaches far beyond the forest’s edge.”

—Shigeki, Jukai visionary
