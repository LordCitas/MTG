El plano de los dioses y semidioses

Los habitantes de Theros trabajan duro para ganarse el pan y el agua de cada día, pero nada de eso les impide rezar y hacer ofrendas a sus dioses patrones. Entidades divinas y etéreas, los dioses supervisan el funcionamiento de Theros y los elementos, cada uno movido por una ideología propia:

Kenrith

Algenus Kenrith, known as the Good King, was the High King of the Realm on Eldraine. He was the husband of Queen Linden Kenrith, and the father of Erec, Hazel, Rowan and Will Kenrith. He was renowned as a canny fighter (only bested by his wife), and was the only person in the Realm to have earned knighthoods at all five courts. Nevertheless, he wasn't a particularly inquisitive or deep-thinking man — he left that to Linden.

Gadwick

Gadwick, the Wizened, is a human wizard of Eldraine, the archmage of Vantress. Unlike the other courts, Vantress has no official ruler. Instead, the people of Vantress are led by the Magic Mirror, Indrelon, itself. However, most of the day-to-day responsibilities tend to fall on one or two notable Vantress loremages like him. It takes a sharp mind to earn the Mirror's respect and decipher its cryptic instructions, so the position of archmage is regarded with a level of respect reserved for the monarchs in other courts.

Ayara

While her official royal title is First of Locthwain, the denizens of the realm also often refer to her as the Widowed Queen, a reference to the many spouses she has wed over the centuries. Ayara weds the champions of Locthwain who seek the Cauldron on the evening before they set out on their ultimately fatal quests. By tradition, a year and a day after a champion sets off on the quest, Ayara declares the champion lost and holds a funeral party. More often than not, the party carries over into the next day, transforming the new dawn into a wedding feast for the queen and her next champion spouse.

Torbran

Dios del fuego, la tierra y la forja, Purphoros es la encarnación del poder creativo del fuego, al igual que su caos y poder destructivo. Valora en gran medida valores como la creatividad, la innovación, la pasión y el fanatismo, ofreciendo bendiciones a artisanos... y también a renegados.
Sus seguidores son tanto lobos de mar, pescadores y comerciantes en busca de corrientes marinas favorables y viajes seguros, como sabios y eruditos que codician conocimientos antiguos y secretos.
